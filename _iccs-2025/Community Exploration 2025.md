<h2><strong>About Community Exploration 2025</strong></h2>
<p>The Community Exploration is an immersive programme that gives local and
international delegates a firsthand look at Singapore’s approach to multicultural
and interreligious harmony. By engaging with local communities and exploring
Singapore’s diverse neighborhoods, delegates will gain invaluable insights
into how social cohesion is fostered and how diverse faith and cultural
practices enrich life in a vibrant, multicultural society.</p>
<p>Community Exploration 2025 will be held in the evenings of 24 and 25 June
2025 after the day’s proceedings, with 4 concurrent sessions per day.</p>

Singapore's oldest Hindu temple, visitors will witness the Dravidian architecture
and participate in understanding Hindu rituals and traditions. Just a short
walk away, Masjid Jamae Chulia, one of the earliest mosques in Singapore,
offers a contrast with its Islamic architecture and history of the Muslim
community in Singapore.</p>

<p>At the Singapore Chinese Cultural Centre, the learning journey will uncover
the unique Chinese Singaporean culture. Participants will engage with exhibitions
that reveal the traditions, practices, and contributions of the Chinese
community in Singapore. The centre's programs and activities aim to foster
greater appreciation of the Chinese arts and culture that have shaped the
nation's multicultural tapestry.</p>

<p>Our Tampines Hub is a vibrant community and lifestyle destination that

community spaces in fostering social harmony and inclusivity.</p>

<li>
<p>Kampong Gelam Heritage District is a historical gem that stands as a testament
to Singapore's Malay heritage and its early Islamic history. The learning
journey through this vibrant district will immerse visitors in the rich
tapestry of Malay culture, arts, and traditions. This visit to Kampong
Gelam will highlight the importance of preserving cultural heritage while
embracing modernity, and how it contributes to the multicultural landscape
of Singapore.</p>

<li>
<p>National Gallery Singapore is a treasure trove of Southeast Asian art,
housed in two national monuments. The learning journey here will cover
the world's largest public collection of Singaporean and Southeast Asian
art, spanning the 19th century to present day. Visitors will gain an understanding
of the region's rich cultural heritage through the visual arts, and how
it has evolved over time.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>

</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">

<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The Indian Heritage Centre offers a deep dive into the rich history and
culture of the Indian community in Singapore. Visitors will explore four
floors of interactive exhibits, showcasing artifacts and stories from the
diaspora's past to the present. The journey will cover the contributions
of the Indian community to Singapore's nation-building and provide insights
into traditional Indian arts, such as dance and music.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>

</div>
<p>(Photo credit: One Punggol)</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>One Punggol is set to be a landmark for community engagement and integration.
The learning journey will showcase how this development brings together
residents of different backgrounds through its integrated community and
lifestyle facilities. Participants will learn about the innovative approaches
to community building and the promotion of a kampung spirit in a modern
setting.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>

<li>
<p>The Community Law Centre at Tian De Temple represents a unique blend of
legal aid within a religious institution. Visitors will learn about the
temple's history and its role in the community, as well as how the law
centre provides accessible legal services and promotes understanding of
legal rights among residents. This visit will highlight the collaboration
between religious organizations and social services to support the community.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
